Where It Begins

Mauldslie Farm, Clyde Valley

The Farm

A Working Scottish Estate With Deep Roots

Mauldslie Farm sits in the Clyde Valley in Lanarkshire — a working estate with a long history of farming, land stewardship, and connection to the natural rhythms of the Scottish countryside. The land has been managed with care for generations, and the red deer that roam it are part of that story.

The farm's herd of red deer — Scotland's largest native land mammal — are free to roam across the estate's fields and woodland. They are not farmed for antler. They live as they would in the wild, shedding naturally each spring as new growth begins.

01
No Harm

The Shed, Not the Stag

Red deer naturally cast their antlers every spring as part of their annual cycle. The old antler simply falls off, and new growth begins almost immediately. We collect only what's been shed — the deer don't feel a thing, and no animal is ever disturbed or interfered with.

02
Traced to Source

Every Piece Has a Known Origin

Because we work directly with Mauldslie Farm, we know exactly where every piece of antler comes from. There's no supply chain, no middlemen, no uncertainty. The antler in your opener was shed on Scottish soil and collected by hand from the same fields.

03
Nothing Wasted

A Material That Would Otherwise Return to the Earth

Cast antler that isn't collected slowly decomposes on the ground — it's calcium-rich and good for the soil, but often goes unnoticed. We give it a second life, turning something fleeting and natural into something you'll keep and use for years.

The Material

Why Antler Is Unlike Anything Else

Antler is the fastest-growing tissue in the animal kingdom. A full rack can grow at up to an inch a day, hardening over the summer and eventually being shed in late winter or early spring. The result is a material that is dense, warm, and full of character — with a grain as individual as a fingerprint.

No two pieces look the same. The curve, the colour, the texture — all determined by the particular deer, the particular year, and the conditions on the hill. When we shape an opener from a piece of shed antler, we work with what the material gives us, not against it.

Care & Shipping

Wipe with a damp cloth after use. Avoid prolonged soaking — antler is a natural material and will thank you for a bit of care. Over time it develops a richer patina, which only adds to its character.

UK delivery within 3–5 working days. Each opener is carefully wrapped and sent in a box with a card describing the antler's origin. International shipping is available at checkout.
