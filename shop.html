The Making Of

From the Highland Hills to Your Kitchen Drawer

Every opener starts as a cast antler found at Mauldslie Farm — a working estate in the Clyde Valley. Deer shed their antlers naturally each spring, and we gather only what's been left on the ground.

Back in the workshop, each piece is assessed for character — the bends, the colour, the weight. It's then cut to length, shaped with files and sandpaper, and fitted with a stainless steel head polished by hand.

There's no production line. Each opener takes time, and we make them in small batches. If you're buying one, you're getting something with real hands on it from start to finish.
